Pricing Guidance

What Does Groundworks Cost in Tipperary?

Every project is different, so we don't publish fixed price lists — that would be misleading. What we can tell you is what drives the cost, so you know what to expect before we visit.

Driveways

Size is the biggest factor. A small single car driveway costs considerably less than a wide double or a sweeping front entrance. Surface material matters too — tarmac sits at the more affordable end, block paving in the middle, and resin bound gravel at the premium end. Existing surface removal, access constraints and sub-base condition all affect the final figure.

Patios

Square meterage drives patio pricing, alongside the material chosen. Budget concrete paving is more affordable; natural stone (sandstone, limestone, granite) or porcelain costs more but delivers a much better long-term result. Steps, raised sections, curved edges and integrated drainage channels add to the price — but also to the quality of the finished space.

Groundworks

General groundworks pricing depends on the scope of excavation, the volume of material to be removed, plant requirements and the nature of the site. Ground conditions in Tipperary vary — rock, clay and made-up ground all behave differently. A straightforward levelling job will cost less than a complex multi-stage site preparation for a new build.

Pipe Laying

Pipe laying costs are influenced by the length of run, pipe diameter, depth of excavation, and whether you need a complete drainage system or a single connection. Soakaways, inspection chambers and road crossings all add to the scope. We'll assess your exact requirements on site and give you a clear, detailed quote before any work begins.

Do I need planning permission for a new driveway or patio in Ireland?

In most cases, residential driveway and patio work in Ireland does not require planning permission under exempted development rules — provided the materials used are permeable or drainage is properly managed so surface water doesn't run onto the public road. We can advise you on this during your free site consultation. For specific circumstances, it's always worth confirming with Tipperary County Council's planning department.

What is the best driveway surface for Irish weather?

It depends on your budget, aesthetic preference and how the driveway is used. Resin bound gravel is increasingly popular — it offers a smart, low-maintenance finish with excellent drainage and holds up well in freeze-thaw cycles. Block paving is durable, highly customisable and individual blocks can be lifted for access to services underneath. Tarmac is the most cost-effective option and handles heavy vehicle use well. We'll walk you through the pros and cons of each during your free quote, so you can make the right call for your situation.

What preparation is needed before a new patio or driveway is laid?

Good preparation is what makes a lasting surface. We excavate to the correct depth for the chosen surfacing material and ground conditions, remove all arisings from site, install a well-compacted granular sub-base, manage any drainage requirements and ensure a stable, even foundation before any surfacing is laid. Cutting corners on prep is the number-one cause of premature surface failure — cracking, sinking and frost heave are almost always a sub-base problem, not a surface problem. We don't cut those corners.
